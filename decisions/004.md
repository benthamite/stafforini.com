## 004: Cite keys must be ASCII alphanumerics (and hyphens) only (2026-04-30)

**Decision:** Cite keys may contain only `[A-Za-z0-9]` plus `-` (for compound surnames like `Stratton-Lake`, `Stephens-Davidowitz`). No accents, periods, underscores, semicolons, or other special characters. `scripts/rename-cite-keys.py` exists to enforce renames safely (updates bib entries + crossref values + `bibliographic-notes/<key>.org` filename and contents + `[cite:@key]` in `~/My Drive/notes/**/*.org` + `work = "<slug>"` in `content/quotes/*.md` + redirect targets, plus appends an old-slug → new-slug redirect).

**Rejected:**
- **Allow non-ASCII letters in cite keys** (the migration's default for `Bródy`, `Müller`, `Encyclopédie`, etc.): produces ugly URL-encoded slugs, breaks tooling that assumes ASCII keys, and the surname stripped of accents is still uniquely identifying for the cite-key purpose.
- **Allow periods for initials and org abbreviations** (`D.Pennebaker`, `GOV.UK`, `U.S.GeologicalSurvey`, `Ready.gov`): same readability/URL issues.
- **Allow underscores and semicolons** (Zotero-style `stavrou_what_2024`, multi-author `DaronAcemoglu;...`): Zotero default but ugly in citations and unsupported by some BibTeX processors.
- **Rename hyphens out of compound surnames too**: the user's existing keys deliberately use them (`Stratton-Lake`); they're standard in BibTeX and not "special" in the rule's intent.

**Files:** `scripts/rename-cite-keys.py`. 31 cite keys renamed across all 6 bib files in this session; 0 special-character keys remain.
