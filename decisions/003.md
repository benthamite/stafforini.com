## 003: Short stories inside books use a dedicated cite key with crossref (2026-04-30)

**Decision:** When a quote is from a specific short story (or chapter / essay) within a book, give the story its own cite key as `@incollection{StoryKey, crossref = {BookKey}, ...}`. The book entry stays as `@book{BookKey, ...}`. Quotes attribute via `:work "<story-slug>"` and `[cite:@StoryKey]`, not the book key.

Example: `Borges1949Aleph` (book), `Borges1949Teologos` and `Borges1949OtraMuerte` (stories within it, each with `crossref = {Borges1949Aleph}`).

**Rejected:**
- **Attribute every quote to the parent book** (the pre-existing pattern in `Borges1949Aleph.org` and friends): hides the structural distinction between book-level quotes (e.g., from an editor's preface or epilogue) and story-level quotes. Sometimes the "book" cite key is even wrong: the "finitude" quote was attributed to `Borges1949Aleph` but is actually from "El tiempo circular" in *Historia de la eternidad* (1936) — a different book entirely.
- **Merge story content into the book entry's `note` field**: structurally lossy, can't render distinct work pages per story, breaks crossref-aware citation rendering.
- **Use Zotero-style `@inbook` everywhere**: BibTeX `@incollection` with crossref is the convention used elsewhere in `new.bib` (Borges1949OtraMuerte, Borges1949BiografiaDeTadeo); stay consistent.

**Files:** `~/My Drive/bibliography/new.bib` (entries with `crossref = {...}`), `~/My Drive/bibliographic-notes/Borges1949Teologos.org` and similar story-level org files.
