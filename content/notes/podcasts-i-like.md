Here's a list of the podcasts I currently subscribe to.


## Favorite {#favorite}

-   [The 80,000 Hours Podcast](https://soundcloud.com/80000-hours). A show about the world's most pressing problems and how you can use your career to solve them.
-   [EconTalk](http://www.econtalk.org/). One-on-one discussions with an eclectic mix of authors, professors, Nobel Laureates, entrepreneurs, leaders of charities and businesses, and people on the street. [Check out listeners' [favorite podcasts](http://www.econtalk.org/archives/favorites/) and Rob Wiblin's [recommended episodes](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1TejVbFNWkAaqRYQ4UaZfwNDKaGGgbUNeGYKJLa7dQ5I/edit?usp=sharing).]
-   [The Turing Test podcast](https://harvardeapodcast.com/). The Harvard Effective Altruism student podcast.
-   [Witness History](https://podcasts.apple.com/ie/podcast/witness-history/id339986758). History as told by the people who were there.


## Also good {#also-good}

-   [Conversations with Tyler](https://conversationswithtyler.com/). Tyler Cowen interviews an eclectic roster of guests.
-   [Hardcore History](https://www.dancarlin.com/hardcore-history-series/). Dan Carlin's ultra-popular podcast on world history.
-   [The Insight](https://insitome.libsyn.com/). Weaving together insights from the fields of genetics, archaeology, linguistics, and paleoanthropology, hosts Spencer Wells and Razib Khan take us on a grand tour of human history.
-   [Making Sense](https://samharris.org/podcast/). A podcast focusing on important and controversial questions about the human mind, society, and current events.
-   [Rationally Speaking](http://rationallyspeakingpodcast.org/). Julia Galef and guests "explore the borderlands between reason and nonsense, likely and unlikely, science and pseudoscience."
-   [Scott H. Young Podcast](https://itunes.apple.com/ca/podcast/scott-h-young-podcast/id1281747833?mt=2). A summary and discussion of each title in Young's monthly book club.
-   [The Way I Heard It](http://mikerowe.com/podcast/). Mike Rowe is perhaps my favorite storyteller. In this podcast, he tells a new short story every week.
