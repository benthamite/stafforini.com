A professor of economics is planning to give a course on effective altruism in the coming academic year, and he asked me and others for a brief list of relevant scholarly writings. Here's what I suggested [I have since revised and updated the list—PS, April 2020]:

- Aschenbrenner, Leopold, [Existential risk and growth](https://leopoldaschenbrenner.github.io/xriskandgrowth/ExistentialRiskAndGrowth050.pdf), forthcoming.
- Beckstead, Nick, *[On the overwhelming importance of shaping the far future](https://docs.google.com/viewer?a=v&pid=sites&srcid=ZGVmYXVsdGRvbWFpbnxuYmVja3N0ZWFkfGd4OjM4ZDVlYjY4ZTkzMzFlZjE)*, unpublished doctoral dissertation, Rutgers University, chap. 1.
- Bostrom, Nick, [Existential risk prevention as global priority](http://www.existential-risk.org/concept.pdf), *Global policy*, vol. 4, no. 1 (February, 2013), pp. 15-31.
- Greaves, Hilary, [Cluelessness](http://stafforini.com/docs/Greaves%20-%20Cluelessness.pdf), *Proceedings of the Aristotelian Society*, vol. 116, no. 3 (December, 2016), pp. 311-339.
- Greaves, Hilary & William MacAskill, [The case for strong longtermism](https://globalprioritiesinstitute.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/Greaves_MacAskill_strong_longtermism.pdf), forthcoming.
- MacAskill, William, [Replaceability, career choice, and making a difference](https://www.academia.edu/1557895/Replaceability_Career_Choice_and_Making_a_Difference), *Ethical theory and moral practice*, vol. 17, no. 2 (April, 2014), pp. 269-283.
- Matheny, Jason Gaverick, [Reducing the risk of human extinction](http://users.physics.harvard.edu/~wilson/pmpmta/Mahoney_extinction.pdf), *Risk analysis*, vol. 27, no. 5 (October, 2007), pp. 1335-1344.
- Matheny, Jason Gaverick and Kai M. A. Chan, [Human diets and animal welfare: the illogic of the larder](http://www.jgmatheny.org/matheny%20chan%202005.pdf), *Journal of agricultural and environmental ethics*, vol. 18, no. 6 (December, 2005), pp. 579–594.
- Ord, Toby, [The moral imperative towards cost-effectiveness in global health](http://amirrorclear.net/files/the-moral-imperative-towards-cost-effectiveness-in-global-health.pdf), Washington DC: The Centre for Global Development, 2013.
- Singer, Peter, [Famine, affluence, and morality](http://pages.uoregon.edu/koopman/courses_readings/singer_famine_affluence_morality.pdf), *Philosophy & public affairs*, vol. 1, no. 3 (Spring, 1972), pp. 229-243.
- Singer, Peter, [All animals are equal](http://www.stafforini.com/txt/Singer - All animals are equal.pdf), in P. Singer (ed.) *Applied ethics*, Oxford: Oxford University Press, 1986, pp. 215-228.
- Trammell, Phil, [Discounting for patient philanthropists](https://philiptrammell.com/static/discounting_for_patient_philanthropists.pdf), forthcoming.

*With thanks to Bastian Stern, Nick Beckstead and Lila Rieber for valuable feedback.*
