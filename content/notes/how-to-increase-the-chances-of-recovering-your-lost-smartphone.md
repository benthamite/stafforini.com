Before reading any further, I'd like to make a request.  If you decide to act on my advice and, after losing your phone, you succeed in recovering it, I'd like to ask you to show me some gratitude and make to a cost-effective charity (e.g. GiveDirectly), meta-charity (e.g. GiveWell) or high-impact research institute (e.g. FHI).

- **Set your wallpaper to display a picture of a smiling baby.** Research by Richard Wiseman suggests that wallets containing a picture of a cute, smiling baby prominently displayed on the inside were several times more likely to be returned that wallets containing no pictures.

:   A cute baby

- **Set your custom carrier label to display your name and email address.** Giving the person who finds your phone your contact details eliminates the [trivial inconvenience](http://lesswrong.com/lw/f1/beware_trivial_inconveniences/) of having to do a bit of extra work to know how to find you.  It also makes it harder for him or her to rationalize the decision to keep the phone.
- **Download and install Lookout for [Android](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.lookout&hl=en) or [iPhone](https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/lookout-backup-security-find/id434893913?mt=8).**
