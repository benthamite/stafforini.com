1. **Express gratitude** (Lyubomirsky & Kurtz 2008, 30-37)

- *Keep a gratitude journal*(Lyubomirsky & Kurtz 2008, 32-34, 36-37; Emmons & McCullough 2003). Once a week, ...
- *Write gratitude letters* (Lyubomirsky & Kurtz 2008 34-35, 37; Sheldon & Lyubomirsky 2006). Pick a family member, friend or acquaintance to whom you feel grateful but have never properly thanked. Write them a letter, describing in detail what they have done for you. If you feel comfortable, send them the letter.

**2. Cultivate optimism** (Lyubomirsky & Kurtz 2008, 38-44)

- *Imagine your best possible self*(Lyubomirsky & Kurtz 2008, 38-44; King 2001; Sheldon & Lyubomirsky 2006). Once a week, spend 20 minutes thinking about how your life would be in 10 years if everything has gone as well as it possibly could. Write a detailed description of what your life would be like under these imagined circumstances.

1. **Avoid rumination and social comparison** (Lyubomirsky & Kurtz 2008, 47-51; Nolem-Hoeksema 1991)

- *Simply stop* (Lyubomirsky & Kurtz 2008, 50). When you catch yourself ruminating, recognize that a cycle is beginning and that it is unlikely to be productive.
- *Schedule time for ruminating*(Lyubomirsky & Kurtz 2008, 51). Set aside a limited period of time --when you are not particularly anxious-- to write our your thoughts or talk about the issue with a trusted friend.
- *Look at the big picture* (Lyubomirsky & Kurtz 2008, 51). Ask yourself, "Will this *really* matter a year from now?" Chances are, it won't.

1. **Practice acs of kindness** (Lyubomirsky & Kurtz 2008, 52-58)

- *Keep a kindness journal**(Lyubomirsky & Kurtz 2008, 57). Perform five acts of kindness each week, varying them as much as possible, and write down he details of these activities at the end of the day you did them.*
- *Engage in prosocial spending* (Lyubomirsky & Kurtz 2008, 57-58; Dunn, Ankin & Norton 2008). Spend some of your discretionary income on other people.

1. **Nurture interpersonal relationships**(Lyubomirsky & Kurtz 2008, 59-

*With colleagues*(Lyubomirsky & Kurtz 2008, 59-60)

- *Keep a mental tally of the things you like about your co-workers.*
- *Try to appreciate the strengths of those who are very different from you.*

*With friends*(Lyubomirsky & Kurtz 2008, 60)

- *Become a good listener.*
- *Engage in honest self-disclosure**.*
- *Be positive, supportive, and loyal**.*

*With lovers*(Lyubomirsky & Kurtz 2008, 60)

- *Share your thoughts and feelings*. For at least 15 minutes each day, talk about your reality as it exists outside of the home.
- *Be positive and supportive*.
- *Appreciate your partner's positive qualities.*Take time to really think about what you like about your partner.
- *Do something novel and exciting together*.
